What is Burni?

Burni is more than just a token. It’s a promise for a deflationary future. At Burni’s core is a mechanism that permanently removes tokens from circulation to potentially increase the value of the remaining tokens.

The Secret of Token Burning

Imagine tokens being burned like logs in a magical fire. They disappear forever! This process, called "token burning," reduces the total supply of Burni tokens. Fewer tokens can mean each one becomes more valuable, similar to rare collectibles.

This animation illustrates how tokens are symbolically removed from circulation.

Burni’s Promise: "Blackholed"

Burni is marked as "Blackholed: YES". This means that the maximum supply of Burni tokens is fixed and no new tokens can ever be created. It’s like throwing away the key to the vault! This is a strong signal of our commitment to a truly deflationary and secure ecosystem from the start.

What does "Blackholed" mean? When a token issuer is "blackholed", it means the issuing address has renounced its rights to mint new tokens or change token properties. This makes the maximum supply truly fixed.

Burni Coin: Deflationary Schedule

We believe in transparency and the long-term value development of Burni Coin. A key component of our ecosystem is the unique deflationary mechanism that continuously reduces the total amount of Burni Coins in circulation. This process creates scarcity and potentially supports the value of the remaining coins.

Key Insights from the Simulation

Based on our calculation, starting with 500,000 coins before the first process, the coins are effectively gone when less than 1 coin remains. This will be the case after 260 processes.

  • Start of the first process: June 1, 2025 (Sunday)
  • End date (after 260 processes, Coins < 1): July 21, 2027 (Wednesday)
  • Total Processes: 260
  • Days elapsed until end: 780 days

The Process in Detail

Starting June 1, 2025, a two-step process will be performed every three days. The values shown here reflect the state after each respective process:

  • Burn: First, 3% of the currently circulating coins are permanently removed from circulation and destroyed.
  • Lock: Then, 2% of the coins remaining after burning are locked. These locked coins are also no longer in active circulation and contribute to reducing the circulating supply.

This cycle repeats every three days until the number of coins falls below a whole unit (less than 1 coin remains).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Enter a search term to filter the FAQs.

1. How do I buy Burni Coin?

Burni Coin can be traded on decentralized exchanges (DEXs) like XPMarket. You need an XRPL-compatible wallet and must set up a Trustline for Burni.

2. What is a "Trustline" and why do I need it?

A Trustline is a feature on the XRP Ledger that allows you to hold non-XRP tokens (like Burni Coin) in your wallet. It confirms that you trust a specific issuer and is required to receive or send Burni. Learn more.

3. What makes Burni Coin unique?

Burni Coin stands out due to its deflationary mechanism (token burning) and its foundation on the fast and efficient XRP Ledger. We also prioritize community governance and the development of real-world use cases.

4. Is Burni Coin secure?

Burni Coin operates on the secure XRP Ledger. The smart contract is designed to ensure tamper-proof security. However, we always recommend conducting your own research and only investing in projects you understand.

5. Where can I join the Burni community?

You can find us on X (Twitter) at @burnicoin, as well as on Telegram and Discord.
